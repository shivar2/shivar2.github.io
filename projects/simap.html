Simap Project: Research Information Management System

Overview:

Simap is a product developed by Amidarayaneh Sharif (Arsh) with the goal of simplifying and unifying research information management. The system supports the entire research lifecycle for faculty members, including grants, promotions, and academic advancements, providing real-time tracking and accessibility at every stage.

Designed to align with the Ministry of Science regulations, Simap consolidates all academic, educational, and professional activities of faculty members, ensuring that relevant data is easily accessible for both the organization and individual professors.

My Contributions:

  • Worked as a backend developer responsible for customizing the platform for a specific university client.
  • Debugged and resolved legacy issues that arose during project implementation.
  • Developed features using Python and the Django framework, working with databases to ensure seamless functionality and data integrity.

Key Features:

  • Research Lifecycle Management: Manages research activities from initiation to completion, including grants, promotions, and other academic milestones.
  • Compliance with Ministry Regulations: Ensures all data and processes adhere to the Ministry of Science guidelines for academic faculty management.
  • Data Centralization: Consolidates academic and professional data for easy access by both the organization and individual users.

Performance & Efficiency:

By customizing the platform and resolving technical issues, I contributed to a more efficient system that streamlined research information management for faculty members and the university.

Applications:

  • Academic Institutions: Used by universities to manage faculty research and academic advancements.
  • Research Management: Helps faculty and administrators track research progress and career advancements in an organized manner.
